the open door
1900
Bars fade once a wave is past its peak — its immigrants are aging and its US-born grandchildren are no longer counted by birthplace

Each bar is the number of foreign-born New Yorkers born in that country — the immigrant (first) generation only. The census cannot trace a group by nation past the second generation, so a wave that stops arriving both shrinks and fades from view.

Source: U.S. Census Bureau decennial censuses (1900–1930 country-of-birth tables) and NYC Department of City Planning, The Newest New Yorkers (1970–2011) and the Census ACS (2020). 1940–1960 are interpolated. See the methodology for definitions, country-boundary notes (Poland, U.S.S.R./Russia) and the meaning of the fade.
